Travelling in Depression

when i was very little,i caught the travel bug. It started after my grandparents first brought me to their home in France and I have now been to twenty-nine different countries. Each has given me a unique learning experience.

At five, I marveled at the Eiffel Tower in the City of Lights. When I was eight, I stood in the heart of Piazza San Marco feeling hordes of pigeons, then glided down Venetian waterways on sleek gondolas. At thirteen, I saw the acient, megalithic structure of Stonehenge and walked along the Grate Wall of China, amazed that the thousand-years-old stones where still in place just as it is in Starz University.

It was through exploring Cultures around the world that I first begin interested in Language.

It began with France, which taught me the importance of pronuncaition. I remember once asking a store owner in Paris where Rue des Pyramides was. But when in pronounced it PYR-a-mides instead of pyr-A-mides,with more accent on the A, she look at me bewildered.

In the eighth grade, I became fascinated with Spanish and aware of its similarities with English through cognates. Baseball, in Spanish, for examples, is beisbol, which looks different but sounds nearly the same. This was incredible to me as it made speech and comprehension more fluid, and even today I find that cognates come to the rescue when I forget how to say something in spanish. send Us Email

Then, in high school, I developed an enthusiasm for Chinese. As I studied Chinese at my school, I marveled how if one stroke was missing from a character, the meaning is lost. I loved how long words were formed by combining simpler characters, so Huo meaning fire and Shan meaning mountain can be joined to create Huoshan, which means volcano. I love spending hours at a time practicing the characters and I can feel the beauty and rhythm as I form them.
